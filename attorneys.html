Philip C. Barth III

Partner

Mr. Barth, grandson of the firm’s founder, is an experienced trial attorney who has tried cases for over twenty-five years. After graduating from the State University of New York at Buffalo Law School, Mr. Barth served as one of the most successful in-house trial attorneys at Fireman’s Fund Insurance Company. Since joining Barth Condren in 1996, he has continued to amass an impressive record of trial and appellate successes. Mr. Barth has been an active member of the Defense Research Institute for many years and was nominated and approved for membership in the prestigious International Association of Defense Counsel (IADC) in 2004. He continues to litigate regularly, specializing in motor vehicle and premises liability.

John R. Condren

Partner

Mr. Condren is an experienced trial attorney in the areas of insurance defense litigation and insurance coverage. He is a 1988 graduate of the State University of New York at Buffalo, having received a B.A. in Psychology. In 1992, Mr. Condren received his J.D. from the University of Toledo College of Law in Toledo, Ohio. For upwards of 30 years, he has regularly appeared in the New York State and Federal District Courts at the trial and appellate levels and has taken numerous cases to verdict over the course of his career. He is also a frequent lecturer and presenter on topics involving personal injury litigation and insurance coverage.

Mr. Condren also serves as a Trial Team Instructor/Coach at the University of Buffalo School of Law Trial Advocacy Program where he is involved in mock trial tournaments throughout the United States.

Finally, Mr. Condren serves as legal counsel and Executive Board Member of the 11 Day Power Play, Inc. – a New York not-for-profit corporation formed for developing and organizing community events for cancer-related causes in the Buffalo, NY area.

Pierre A. Vincent

Senior Trial Attorney

Mr. Vincent is an experienced trial and appellate attorney who has tried cases for over twenty years. He earned his J.D. from Southwestern University School of Law in 1995, his B.A. in History from Williams College in 1986 and his A.S in Public Communications from Mount Wachusett Community College in 1983. Mr. Vincent is experienced in employment litigation, including investigating and litigating claims of workplace discrimination, sexual harassment and wrongful termination. He has responded to employment related charges to government agencies including the NLRB, EEOC and DHR, as well as litigating claimed violations of New York General Municipal Law and Civil Service Law.
